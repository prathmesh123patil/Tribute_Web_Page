A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam BR ( 15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and aerospace engineering. He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and was intimately involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile development efforts. He thus came to be known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first since the original nuclear test by India in 1974

Biographies

  • "Wings of Fire: An Autobiography" by Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam - This is Dr. Kalam's own autobiography, which provides insights into his early life, education, and his journey to becoming a renowned scientist and India's 11th President.
  • "Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India" by Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam - In this book, Dr. Kalam discusses his vision for a prosperous and developed India and how the country's youth can contribute to achieving that vision.
  • "My Journey: Transforming Dreams into Actions" by Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam - Another autobiography of Dr. Kalam, this book delves deeper into his experiences and aspirations, emphasizing the importance of determination and hard work.
  • "The Luminous Sparks" by Abdul Kalam and Yagnaswami Sundara Rajan - This book explores Dr. Kalam's interactions with various people and how these interactions influenced his life and work.
